Research Context

Why this research matters

🏛️ The Repository

Sri Lanka possesses 30,000+ unprocessed palm-leaf manuscripts (ඕල පොත්) containing centuries of Ayurvedic medicinal knowledge.

These texts encode herbal formulations, mineral preparations, and therapeutic procedures — many involving highly toxic ingredients that require specific purification.

🎯 Research Gap

  • No prior NLP work on archaic/classical Sinhala
  • Existing Sinhala NLP tools target modern Sinhala only
  • No computational Ayurvedic pharmacovigilance system exists
  • Sinhala classified as "digitally underserved" (Joshi et al., ACL 2020)

⚙️ Weak Supervision Strategy

Manual annotation of 70K archaic Sinhala sentences is infeasible. We use programmatic labelling:

$$\text{label}(w_i) = \begin{cases} \text{STOP} & \text{if } w_i \text{ ends with suffix} \in S \\ \text{STOP} & \text{if } i = |W|-1 \text{ (end of line)} \\ \text{O} & \text{otherwise}\end{cases}$$

Uses 15 morphological suffixes from Sinhala SOV grammar.

Stage 1A: Bigram Language Model

OCR Post-Correction — Training the language model

📖 Noisy Channel Model

Following Shannon's information theory, OCR correction is a decoding problem:

$$W^* = \arg\max_W P(O|W) \cdot P(W)$$

Where $P(O|W)$ = OCR confidence, $P(W)$ = language model probability.

Bigram Probability

$$P(w_2 | w_1) = \frac{\text{Count}(w_1, w_2)}{\text{Count}(w_1)}$$

🔧 Why NOT Deep Learning?

Neural NMT models require millions of parallel examples. With only 70K sentences and no noisy→clean pairs, a bigram model is both sufficient and interpretable. (Rijhwani et al., EMNLP 2020)

Stage 1A: Viterbi Decoder

Dynamic programming for optimal word sequence selection

Scoring Equation

$$V_{t,k} = \max_{x} \Big[ V_{t-1,x} \cdot \big( \alpha \cdot P_{\text{OCR}}(w_t) + \beta \cdot P_{\text{LM}}(w_t | w_{t-1}) \big) \Big]$$
ParameterValueRationale
α (OCR weight)0.6Trust scanner slightly more
β (LM weight)0.4Linguistic context correction
Smoothing ε0.0001Prevent zero-probability paths

Complexity: Reduces $O(S^N)$ → $O(N \cdot S^2)$, yielding ~0.1s latency

Stage 1A: Edge Cases & Decisions

What went wrong and how we fixed it

⚠️ Zero-Probability Paths

Problem: If bigram $(w_1, w_2)$ never appeared in corpus, probability = 0, which collapses the entire Viterbi path score to zero.

Solution: Additive (Laplace) smoothing with $\epsilon = 10^{-4}$ ensures $P(w_2|w_1) > 0$ for all word pairs.

⚠️ Out-of-Vocabulary Words

Problem: If the correct historical word was never seen in training, the decoder cannot emit it.

Trade-off accepted: Closed vocabulary prevents hallucinating non-existent medicinal herbs — a safety feature, not a bug.

🎯 Design Decision: α=0.6, β=0.4

Why trust the OCR scanner more? Under the assumption that OCR confidence scores are calibrated — the scanner's top candidate is correct ~60% of the time for well-preserved regions. The LM corrects the remaining 40% where scanner confidence is low and linguistic context is strong.

The core thesis contribution — sentence boundary detection

THE CORE HYPOTHESIS

"In low-resource scenarios like archaic Sinhala, a lightweight CRF injected with hand-crafted domain-specific morphological rules outperforms data-hungry multilingual transformers (XLM-RoBERTa, 278M params)."

"සිංහල වැනි අවම දත්ත ඇති භාෂාවකදී, ලෝකයේ දියුණුම Deep Learning මොඩලයකට වඩා, අප අතින් භාෂාමය නීති ඇතුළත් කළ කුඩා CRF මොඩලය ඉතා සාර්ථකයි"

Why CRF Over Deep Learning?

Theoretical justification for the architectural choice

CRF Formulation

$$P(\mathbf{y}|\mathbf{x}) = \frac{1}{Z(\mathbf{x})} \exp\!\left(\sum_{i}\sum_{j} \lambda_j f_j(y_{i-1}, y_i, \mathbf{x}, i)\right)$$
  • Models label-label transitions ($y_{i-1} \to y_i$)
  • Consecutive STOPs are rare → CRF learns this naturally
  • Feature functions $f_j$ inject domain knowledge directly

Linguistic Basis

Sinhala = SOV language

Subject-Object-Verb → the predicate (verb) naturally occupies sentence-final position. In Ayurvedic instructional texts, sentences end with specific imperative/declarative verb forms.

Agglutinative Morphology

Tense, mood, aspect markers are appended as suffixes to the verb root → suffix matching provides strong boundary signals.

Shao et al. (TACL 2018): CRFs with morphological features remain competitive for agglutinative languages. Lafferty et al. (ICML 2001): Original CRF paper.

🐛 Critical Bug Found & Fixed

The EOS Position Bias:

train_labeled.tsv has one sequence per line (~12 words). STOP tags appear predominantly at line-end. If trained on these short sequences, CRF learns STOP ≈ EOS and fails on continuous text.

Fix: Load data as a continuous stream, chunk into fixed 30-word windows. STOP tags now appear at arbitrary positions within windows.

This was continuous=True mode in load_data()

Evaluation Circularity Trap

A critical methodological decision that evaluators WILL ask about

⚠️ The Problem

The 852K training labels were generated using the same morphological suffix rules that appear as CRF features.

A standard 80/20 random split would produce ~100% circular accuracy — the model trivially replicates the labelling heuristic!

This is NOT a valid evaluation. Any reviewer would reject this.

✅ Our Resolution

We train on full train_labeled.tsv but evaluate on gold_test.tsv — a separate gold standard with 500 manually-verified sentences.

  • Completely isolated from weak supervision rules
  • Manually validated sentence boundaries
  • Produces realistic metrics: 96.7% accuracy, F1≈0.83
  • Same methodology used in evaluation/evaluate.py

Additionally, our evaluation framework includes: 5-fold CV, ablation studies, baseline comparisons (majority, random, rule-only), McNemar's test, and bootstrap 95% CI

Safety Engineering Edge Cases

Three critical bugs found and fixed during development

1. Substring Match Bug

Problem: Matching "ජයපාල" inside "ජයපාලක්ෂේත්‍ර" (a harmless place name).

Fix: Pad each sentence with spaces: f" {sentence} " and search for f" {term} "

2. Short-Alias Double-Count

Problem: After matching "ජයපාල ඇට", the shorter alias "ජයපාල" fires again on the same text.

Fix: Global masking — replace matched entity with [MASK] after detection.

3. Match Order

Problem: "ජයපාල" matches before the more specific "ජයපාල ඇට", losing the detailed entity info.

Fix: Sort all toxic terms by length descending → longest-match-first.

4. Non-Toxic Noise

Problem: KG contained Low/None/Safe entities that triggered unnecessary STOP checks, creating false positives.

Fix: Filter: skip entities with Toxicity ∈ {Low, None, Safe, No, ""}. Only 1,162 of 2,100 entities are genuinely toxic.

AI acts as collaborative augmented intelligence rather than a brittle autonomous agent.

The Cascading Failure Mode

Why SBD accuracy is a Patient Safety Requirement, not just an NLP metric

The Dependency Chain

Upstream segmentation errors propagate into downstream safety failures:

  • Over-Segmentation (False STOP): Breaks sentence between toxic ingredient and purification → guardrail can't find purification → False Positive (unnecessary rejection)
  • Under-Segmentation (Missed STOP): Merges two recipes → purification from Recipe B validates toxic ingredient in Recipe A → False Negative (dangerous approval)

Known Limitations

Honest acknowledgment — what we cannot do yet

1. Weak Supervision Circularity

Training labels use the same suffix rules as CRF features. Mitigated by gold standard evaluation, but the circularity remains a methodological concern.

2. No Real OCR Integration

Viterbi decoder operates on simulated OCR output with synthetic confidence scores. Real scans may have different error distributions.

3. No Dosage Modelling

Binary toxicity (High/Low) ignores dose-response (Haber's Rule). Small amounts of toxic ingredients may be safe.

4. No Negation Handling

"Do NOT use Niyangala" is flagged identically to "Use Niyangala." Pipeline implemented basic negation heuristic but it's incomplete.

5. KG Completeness

2,100 entities do not cover ALL toxic Ayurvedic ingredients. Entities not in the KG produce silent false negatives.

6. Single-Annotator Gold Set

500 gold test sentences annotated by a single expert. No inter-annotator agreement score available.

These limitations are explicitly documented in the thesis (Chapter 10) and form the basis for Phase 2 improvements.

config.py — Single Source of Truth

All suffix lists, hyperparameters, and paths are centralized to eliminate mismatch bugs:

  • CANONICAL_ENDINGS — 13 suffixes used in CRF features
  • WEAK_SUPERVISION_EXTRA_ENDINGS — 3 additional suffixes used ONLY in label generation (avoids circularity)
  • CRF_PARAMS — L-BFGS, c1=0.1, c2=0.1
  • VITERBI_ALPHA/BETA — 0.6 / 0.4
  • normalize_sinhala() — NFC Unicode normalization

Key Design Decision

WEAK_SUPERVISION_EXTRA_ENDINGS are deliberately separated from CANONICAL_ENDINGS to partially mitigate the circularity concern.
